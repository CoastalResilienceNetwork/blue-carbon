Wetlandsare among the most carbon-rich ecosystemsin the world, and mangroves represent an especially rich source of Blue Carbon (carbon stored and sequestered by coastal and marine ecosystems). Under the Paris Climate Agreement, countries commit to a nationally determined contribution (NDC) which determine and define national climate goals and action plans. Conserving and restoring wetlands such as mangrovesrepresentnatural climate solution (NCS) pathways towards meeting NDC targets.

This app demonstrates the potential role of mangrove NCS in contributing to and enhancing climate mitigation ambition in countries with mangrove habitats. Contributions from aboveground biomass (from Simard et al., 2019) and soil (Sanderman et al., 2018)are summed to derive the total mangrove carbon storage by country. The Blue Carbon opportunity from mangroves represents the Blue Carbon that can be gained on an annual basis through restoration efforts plus the Blue Carbon from annual avoided loss. These values are derived from mangrove extent estimates from Global Mangrove Watch. Protected area data are from World Database of Protected Areas (UNEP-WCMC and IUCN 2018).See this document for more detailed methodology and sources.
