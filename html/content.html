View Mangrove Carbon Storage

Total mangrove carbon storage represents the sum of the aboveground carbon values and the soil organic carbon values attributed to mangrove forests. Values are expressed as Mg (Megagrams) per square kilometer. 1 Megagram is equal to 1 metric tonne.

Above-ground estimates of mangrove carbon are derived from Hutchison et al. (2013). This model used field data on mangrove storage and fluxes from around the world, based on a literature search. Two sets of climatic variables, temperature and precipitation, were then used as predictors to develop a global model of above-ground biomass. While the original study projected this model over a mangrove map developed by Spalding et al. (2010), for the purpose of this analysis, the model was re-projected over the Giri et al. (2011) mangrove map, using the methodology described in Sanderman et al. (2018) to ensure coverage consistent with the below-ground dataset. Conversion factors to convert mangrove biomass to carbon values range from .45 to .50. (Howard et al. 2014). For this analysis, a mean conversion value of .475 was used after Hamilton and Friess (2016).

Soil organic carbon data are from Sanderman et al. (2018), which employed a machine-learning statistical model applied to a database of soil carbon measurements, resulting in a model of soil carbon density. This model was then projected over a dataset of global mangrove distribution from the year 2000 (Giri et al. 2011).

Nationally Determined Commitment (NDC) refers to the committed emission reduction as submitted to the Paris Climate Agreement. Values are expressed in metric tonnes of carbon per year.

Percent of mangrove within a protected area is based on the total mangrove area within a jurisdiction that fall within a protected area, as defined by the World Database of Protected Areas (UNEP-WCMC and IUCN 2018).
